Sandboxes are built with sturdy posts, beams, and roof; provide shade and most importantly, keeps the sand in the sandbox. The concrete floor is a shallow hole and is no deeper than a specific dog’s underbelly.

Greenhouse plastic panels go a third of the height of the walls to block the sand from escaping. The remaining height of the wall has stronger pet screens installed.

There is a short, marine-grade, no-rust, metal grated pathway up to the entrance to help get the sand out from their paws while walking out. The doorway has removable brushes, mounted to the sides of doorway to match the height of the dog’s sides, to brush off as much of the sand as possible while exiting.

Once finished for the day, zip down the zipper to close the door so the sandbox doesn’t become a litterbox.
